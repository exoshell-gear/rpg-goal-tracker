How To Play

Veronium is a goal-tracking system built to help you accomplish goals that would otherwise feel impossible.

Turn your life into a video game, fight dragons, earn rewards, and accomplish your goals.

You move everything by hand on purpose. Nothing happens unless you decide it happened.

Step 1 — Add the Dragon (Your Big Goal)

The first thing you should do is add a Dragon.

Right-click on the map → choose Dragon → place it at the end of the path.

The dragon is your big goal. It should feel scary, but not impossible.

A good dragon usually takes 3–6 months.

Examples:

  • Lose 20 lbs
  • Finish a big project
  • Build a habit you keep avoiding

If it makes you a little nervous, you picked the right goal.

Step 2 — Set the Treasure (Your Big Reward)

When you place a dragon, a Treasure (trophy) appears automatically.

Click the treasure and write down how you will reward yourself after you defeat the dragon.

This should be something you normally would not do for yourself.

Examples:

  • A trip
  • A new watch
  • A big celebration

You can only claim this treasure after the dragon is defeated.

Step 3 — Add Your Starting Point

Now add a Start milestone at the beginning of the path.

This is where you are right now. Be honest.

Examples:

  • 20 lbs overweight
  • No gym membership yet
  • Idea only, no action taken

This is not about judging yourself. It’s just the starting line.

Step 4 — Build the Path

Now fill in the space between the start and the dragon.

The easiest way is to work backwards from the dragon.

Ask yourself:

“What has to happen before I can reach this?”

Each answer becomes a milestone.

Example:

  • Lose 20 lbs (Dragon)
  • Lose 10 lbs
  • Go to the gym regularly
  • Get a workout plan
  • Get a gym membership
  • 20 lbs overweight (Start)

Milestones vs Enemies

Milestones are normal steps.

Use them for things that need to be done, but aren’t scary.

Examples:

  • Buy gym clothes
  • Sign up for something
  • Read a book

Enemies are things you are avoiding.

Use an enemy when the task is something you:

  • Don’t want to do
  • Keep putting off
  • Feel nervous about

Example:

  • Your first day at the gym

Enemies represent fear or resistance — not difficulty.

Enemy Rewards (Small Treats)

When you add an enemy, a small reward appears automatically.

This is because most people forget to reward themselves.

These rewards should be:

  • Small
  • Fun
  • Guilt-free

Examples:

  • Watch your favorite show
  • Eat your favorite food
  • Take a night off

You earn these by defeating enemies.

Tasks Inside a Milestone

Tasks are things that must be done before you move forward.

They help keep steps in the right order.

Example:

You can’t go to the gym before you get a gym membership.

Moving Your Character

Your character represents you.

Move your character when:

  • You complete a milestone
  • You defeat an enemy
  • You want to recognize progress

There are no rules. It’s a visual reminder, not a score.

How Often to Use This

As much or as little as you want.

You can use this daily, weekly, or only when you feel stuck.

There is no “correct” schedule.

If You Do Something Wrong

You can’t fail this.

Nothing is locked. Nothing is permanent.

If your goal changes, change the map.

If you move things too early, move them back.

This is not a test.

The only wrong way to use this is to stop being honest with yourself.
